Molecular Biology

Molecular biology, field of science concerned with studying the chemical structures and processes of biological phenomena that involve the basic units of life, molecules. Of growing importance since the 1940s, molecular biology developed out of the related fields of biochemistry, genetics, and biophysics. The discipline is particularly concerned with the study of proteins and nucleic acidsâ€”i.e., the macromolecules that are essential to life processes. Molecular biology seeks to understand the three-dimensional structure of these macromolecules through such techniques as X-ray diffraction and electron microscopy.

The discipline particularly seeks to understand the molecular basis of genetic processes; molecular biologists map the location of genes on specific chromosomes, associate these genes with particular characters of an organism, and use recombinant DNA technology to isolate, sequence, and modify specific genes.
